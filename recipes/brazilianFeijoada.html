Brazilian Feijoada

Feijoada is a black bean and pork stew that Brazilians often serve topped with farofa, toasted cassava flour. Many call this comfort food the national dish of Brazil.

Ingredients

  • black beans
  • slab bacon
  • pork ribs
  • mexican chorizo sausages
  • smoked sausage
  • large onion
  • cloves garlic
  • tomatoes
  • salt

Steps

  1. In a large bowl with water, soak beans overnight
  2. Add the oil and bacon, cook until crisp and transfer to a plate
  3. Brown ribs and sausages in batches.
  4. On medium-high, saut√© onion, and garlic until soft and translucent, about 5 minutes. Add tomatoes and cook for another 3 minutes.
  5. Drain and rinse the soaked beans. Add them to the pot along with the ribs, bacon, sausages, salt, pepper, and bay leaf.
  6. Bring the mixture to a boil and reduce the heat to low.
  7. Serve with white rice and sprinkle some farofa on top.
